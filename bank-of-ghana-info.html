Bank of Ghana Information

Understanding Ghana's central bank and banking regulations

About the Bank of Ghana

The Bank of Ghana (BoG) is the central bank of Ghana and is responsible for the monetary policy and financial stability of the country. Established in 1957, the Bank of Ghana serves as the nation's primary financial authority and regulator.

As a regulated financial institution, GhanaTrust Bank operates under the supervision and guidelines set forth by the Bank of Ghana to ensure the safety and security of our customers' funds and the stability of Ghana's financial system.

Key Functions of the Bank of Ghana

  • Formulating and implementing monetary policy to maintain price stability
  • Issuing and regulating the Ghanaian currency (the Ghana Cedi - GH¢)
  • Licensing and supervising banks and other financial institutions
  • Promoting the development of Ghana's financial system
  • Managing the country's foreign exchange reserves
  • Acting as banker and financial advisor to the government
  • Promoting financial inclusion and literacy across Ghana

Your Protection as a Customer

Deposit Protection

The Bank of Ghana ensures that all licensed banks maintain adequate capital and liquidity to protect depositors' funds. GhanaTrust Bank adheres to all regulatory requirements to safeguard your deposits.

Regulatory Oversight

Regular inspections and audits by the Bank of Ghana ensure that GhanaTrust Bank operates in compliance with all banking regulations and best practices.

Consumer Protection

The Bank of Ghana has established consumer protection guidelines that GhanaTrust Bank follows to ensure fair treatment of all customers and transparent banking practices.

Ghana Association of Banks

GhanaTrust Bank is a proud member of the Ghana Association of Banks (GAB), the industry body that represents the interests of banks in Ghana. GAB works closely with the Bank of Ghana to develop policies that promote a stable and efficient banking sector.

Through our membership in GAB, we stay informed about industry developments, best practices, and regulatory changes that benefit our customers and the broader Ghanaian economy.

Important Banking Regulations

GhanaTrust Bank operates in full compliance with key banking regulations including:

  • Banks and Specialized Deposit-Taking Institutions Act, 2016 (Act 930)
  • Companies Act, 2019 (Act 992)
  • Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843)
  • Anti-Money Laundering Act, 2020 (Act 1044)
  • Bank of Ghana's regulatory notices and directives

Commitment to Compliance

At GhanaTrust Bank, we are committed to maintaining the highest standards of regulatory compliance. Our dedicated compliance team ensures that we adhere to all Bank of Ghana regulations and international best practices in banking operations.

We regularly update our policies and procedures to reflect changes in the regulatory environment, ensuring that our customers always receive services that meet or exceed regulatory requirements.
